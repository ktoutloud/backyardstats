This week I learned that the quantile function in R has nine different methods for calculating quantiles. This caused a bit of confusion at work when I needed to match summaries from SAS. (Admittedly, I get confused every time I have to match R and SAS output.)

Hereâ€™s an example of the 9 methods (default is #7).

values <- c(1, 2, 3, 4)
sapply(1:9, function(x) quantile(values, probs = c(0.25, .5, 0.75), type = x))
##     [,1] [,2] [,3] [,4] [,5] [,6] [,7]     [,8]   [,9]
## 25%    1  1.5    1    1  1.5 1.25 1.75 1.416667 1.4375
## 50%    2  2.5    2    2  2.5 2.50 2.50 2.500000 2.5000
## 75%    3  3.5    3    3  3.5 3.75 3.25 3.583333 3.5625

Wikipedia has a nice table that shows how the methods line up between R, SAS, Maple, Stata, ScipPy, Julia and even Excel.
